# Client Walkthrough Script

Use this as a short screen-share path for the demo. Keep the tone practical: this is a public website that makes the league easier for families to use while official registration and records stay in PlayMetrics or the approved league platform.

## Before The Call

- Replace the PlayMetrics organization-finder fallback in `registrationLink` with the league's direct PlayMetrics registration URL when the board provides it.
- Confirm the league name, season dates, field names, and sponsor names that are safe to show.
- Keep pricing, sponsor fees, and proposal numbers out of the browser window.
- Open the homepage and keep the registration section ready in a second tab or anchor link: `/#registration`.
- If the direct PlayMetrics URL is not available yet, say that the demo is using PlayMetrics' public organization finder as a temporary fallback.

## Opening Positioning

"This demo is meant to be the league's public front door. It helps families find the right next step quickly, while registration, waivers, payments, and official account records stay in PlayMetrics or the approved league platform."

Use that framing before showing details. It prevents the board from hearing the site as a replacement for their league platform.

## Two-Minute Walkthrough

1. Start with the hero.
   "This gives families the main answer right away: registration is open, the season is active, and baseball and softball information is in one place."

2. Point to the quick actions.
   "Parents usually arrive with a task. These tiles send them to registration, schedule details, field status, or volunteer steps without making them search through a portal."

3. Open the registration section.
   "This does not replace PlayMetrics. The website explains what families need, then sends them into the approved registration platform for signups, waivers, and account records."

4. Show the season hub.
   "The board can publish the dates families ask about most: evaluations, teams, opening day, pictures, and current announcements."

5. Use the field status filters.
   "This is the phone-at-the-field feature. Families can quickly check whether games are on before they leave home."

6. Show sponsors and volunteers.
   "The site also helps with the board's ongoing work: sponsor recognition, volunteer recruiting, and clear next steps for families who want to help."

7. End on the FAQ and footer note.
   "The site stays clear about its role. Families get a clean public front door, while official league workflows stay in the approved platform."

## Five-Minute Demo Path

Use this version if the client wants a little more detail.

1. Start at the top of the page.
   "The first screen is built for busy parents. They can tell what the league offers, who it serves, and where to go next without logging into a portal first."

2. Click or point through the quick-action tiles.
   "These are the jobs parents usually came to do: register, check dates, check fields, or volunteer. The website keeps those actions visible."

3. Scroll to registration and open the CTA.
   "This button currently points to the PlayMetrics organization finder. When the board provides the league's direct registration or program URL, this one link gets swapped so families land closer to the exact signup flow."

4. Show the registration explainer cards.
   "The website can answer the common pre-registration questions before families enter PlayMetrics: who can play, what to have ready, and what happens after signup."

5. Show season dates and announcements.
   "The board can use this section for high-volume updates like evaluations, team announcements, opening day, picture day, and current reminders."

6. Use the field status filters.
   "This section is designed for quick phone checks. If a field closes, the page can show the status and the coach can still send team-specific details through the approved communication process."

7. Show sponsors, volunteers, FAQ, and footer note.
   "The lower half supports the board's year-round work: sponsor recognition, volunteer recruiting, and clear answers for parent questions. The footer repeats the important boundary that official registration and records stay in the approved platform."

## Transition Framing

"The value here is that the public website is not tightly coupled to a buried registration page. Today it can point families into PlayMetrics. If the league receives a new direct registration URL or changes how programs are listed, the public site can update links, copy, and support content without rebuilding the parent experience."

## If They Ask About The Missing Direct Link

"The demo is intentionally using a safe PlayMetrics fallback until the league confirms the direct registration destination. Once that URL is available, the site has one `registrationLink` value to update, so we do not have to search through the page for scattered links."

## Closing Ask

"The next useful step is to confirm the live registration destination and the public-safe league details: official name, field list, season dates, sponsor names, volunteer contacts, and any approved photos or colors."

## Discovery Prompts

- "Where do parents get stuck most often today?"
- "Who is responsible for updating registration links, field status, and announcements?"
- "What parts of the SportsConnect transition history still matter for records, training, or parent instructions?"
- "What has the league been told about the PlayMetrics timing?"
- "Which assets are already approved for public use: logo, colors, photos, sponsor names, and field addresses?"

## Post-Call Notes To Capture

- Direct PlayMetrics registration or program URL.
- Final league name and any wording the board wants families to see.
- Approved photos, logo, colors, and sponsor names.
- Field status update owner and expected update process.
- Volunteer or board contact destination for public inquiries.
