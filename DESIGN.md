# Design

## Direction

A bright, practical community-sports website with the confidence of a well-run league office and the warmth of a spring evening at the fields.

## Color

- Background: warm off-white, not pure white
- Primary: deep navy for trust and sports structure
- Accent: confident red for primary actions
- Support: grass green for field status and positive state
- Detail: clay tan for subtle baseball texture

Use color deliberately. Red should mark action, green should mark field readiness, and navy should carry navigation and structure.

## Typography

- Display: condensed athletic headline style
- Body: rounded, readable sans-serif
- Labels: short, sturdy, and easy to scan

Headlines should feel energetic without becoming loud. Body copy should stay readable on phones.

## Components

- Header with compact navigation and one primary action.
- Hero with real baseball imagery, clear headline, and two obvious actions.
- Quick-action tiles for parent tasks.
- Season hub with field status, dates, announcements, and division details.
- Sponsor visibility section without public dollar amounts.
- Volunteer and board contact section.
- FAQ accordion for parent questions.

## Layout

Use open sections, strong spacing, and a clear vertical story. Cards are allowed for repeated operational items, but avoid nested cards and repetitive grids. Keep mobile views practical and touch-friendly.

## Motion

Use subtle hover and focus transitions. Avoid attention-seeking motion. Respect reduced-motion preferences.
